Q-3 What is negative infinity?

In JavaScript, negative infinity is a special value representing a mathematical concept of negative infinity. It is often denoted as -Infinity. It is the opposite of positive infinity (Infinity), and both are used to represent values that exceed the upper or lower bounds of the representable numeric values in JavaScript.
=>Example:-
<script>
let negativeInfinity = -Infinity;
console.log(negativeInfinity);
console.log(negativeInfinity + 1);
console.log(1 / negativeInfinity);
</script>
